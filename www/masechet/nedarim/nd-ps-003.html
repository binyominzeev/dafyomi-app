12TH CYCLE DEDICATION
NEDARIM 2-5 - Two weeks of study material have been dedicated by Mrs. Estanne Abraham Fawer to honor the eighth Yahrzeit of her father, Rav Mordechai ben Eliezer Zvi (Rabbi Morton Weiner) Z'L, who passed away on 18 Teves 5760. May the merit of supporting and advancing Dafyomi study -- which was so important to him -- during the weeks of his Yahrzeit serve as an Iluy for his Neshamah.

(c)Question: If so, he should teach about Yados first!

(e)Question: This is like the opinion that Kinuyim are the words Nochrim use for these matters. According to the opinion that Chachamim invented Kinuyim, how can we answer? (Even if this opinion agrees that Kinuyim are mid'Oraisa, they are not known more than Yados!)

1.Every Yad for a Neder is like saying the full Neder. Every Kinuy for a Neder is like saying 'Neder'. The following are Yados - if one said... The following are Kinuyim: Konam, Konach...

2)THE SOURCE FOR YADOS

(a)Question: From which verse do we derive Yados?

(b)Answer #1: We learn from "Ish...Ki Yafli Lindor Neder Nazir Lehazir la'Shem";

1.(Beraisa): "Nazir Lehazir" teaches that Kinuyim and Yados work for Nezirus. (We do not learn Kinuyim from the verse. 'Kinuyim' was taught needlessly (Rosh), or should be deleted from the text (Ran).)

2.Question: This teaches about Nezirus. What is the source regarding Nedarim?

3.Answer: "Neder Nazir" equates Nedarim and Nezirus to each other:

i.Just like Yados work for Nezirus, also for Nedarim;

ii.Just like one who transgresses a Neder or delays fulfilling a Neder (to bring a Korban) transgresses 'Bal Yachel' and 'Bal Te'acher', the same applies to Nezirus;

iii.Just like a father or husband can annul the Nedarim of his daughter or wife, he can annul her Nezirus;

(c)Question: We learned Yados Nezirus from "Nazir Lehazir." We should likewise learn Yados Nedarim from "Lindor Neder"! (Why must we learn from a Hekesh?)

(d)Answer: Had it said "Neder Lindor", this would be correct. "Lindor Neder" is grammatically normal. The Torah speaks as people do; we do not expound it.

(e)Question: This is like the opinion that the Torah speaks as people do (and we need not expound it);

1.According to the opinion that disagrees (and expounds every extra word), what do we learn from "Lindor Neder"?

(f)Answer (and Answer #2 to Question (a)): It teaches that Yados of Nedarim are like Nedarim;

1.The Hekesh teaches that Yados of Nezirus are like Nezirus;

2.He expounds "Nazir Lehazir" to teach that if a Nazir accepted Nezirus, it takes effect (he must fulfill another term of Nezirus after his current Nezirus).

3)ISURIM LEARNED FROM NEDARIM TO NEZIRUS

(a)Question: Granted, one can transgress (only) Bal Yachel of Nedarim if he vowed to eat a loaf and did not eat it;

1.How can one transgress (only) Bal Yachel of Nezirus?

2.If he eats (grapes), he (also) transgresses Bal Yochal (the Isur to eat). If he drinks wine, he (also) transgresses Bal Yishteh (the Isur to drink)!

(b)Answer (Rava): He also transgresses Bal Yachel for Nezirus.

(c)Question: How can Bal Te'acher apply to Nezirus?

1.Once he says 'I am a Nazir', (he is immediately a Nazir.) If he eats (grapes), he transgresses Bal Yochal. If he drinks wine, he transgresses Bal Yishteh!

(d)Answer #1: The case is, he said "When I want, I will be a Nazir."

(e)Objection: But in such a case, there is never Bal Te'acher!

(f)Answer #2 (Rava): It applies when he said 'I will be a Nazir before I die.' He is (obligated to become) a Nazir immediately;

1.This is like a Kohen who gave a Get and said that it will take effect just before he dies. His wife is immediately forbidden to eat Terumah, lest he die right away;

2.Also here, he is (obligated to become) a Nazir immediately, lest he die right away!
