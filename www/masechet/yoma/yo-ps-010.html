2)

MEZUZAH IN THE MIKDASH

(a)

Only the Lishkas Parhadrin (the residence of the Kohen Gadol) must have a Mezuzah.

(b)

Question: But other Lishkos (also residences) did not?

(c)

Answer: It is a Gezeirah (lest the Kohen Gadol be considered incarcerated).

(d)

Question: Why isn't residence enough to obligate?

(e)

Answer (Rava): R. Yehudah holds that only year-round residences are obligated in a Mezuzah.

(f)

Question (Abaye): But we find winter/summer homes called Bayis?

(g)

Answer: They are called winter-homes, not simply homes.

(h)

Question: But R. Yehudah obligates a Mezuzah in a Sukah?

1.

There could hardly be a more seasonal dwelling.

2.

And if you'll suggest that the obligation is only de'Rabanan...

10b----------------------------------------10b

3.

But he also makes the produce brought directly into the Sukah subject to Ma'aser (and this we would not do unless the obligation was d'Oraisa, for fear of errors).

(i)

Answer (Abaye): They agree that Sukah is obligated in Mezuzah during the week of Sukos; they argue about whether (Chachamim) or not (R. Yehudah) to extend that obligation to the rest of the year.

(j)

Question (Rava): But the Mishnah teaches that the Chachamim argue about Mezuzah during Sukos!?

(k)

Answer (Rava): Rather, there is no argument during the year (exempt) and they only argue during Sukos.

(l)

Sukah and Lishkah have separate bases for the argument.

1.

(Sukah): R. Yehudah holds that Sukah must be of a permanent nature; Rabanan hold it must be a temporary dwelling.

2.

(Lishkah): Chachamim hold that an imposed dwelling is called a dwelling; R. Yehudah holds it is not (and the obligation for a Mezuzah, as mentioned, is a Gezeirah).
