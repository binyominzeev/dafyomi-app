1)MELACHAH SHE'EINAH TZERICHAH L'GUFAH AND DAVAR SHE'EINO MISKAVEN

(a)Inference: Shmuel holds like R. Shimon (who permits Davar she'Eino Miskaven).

(b)Question: But Shmuel holds that we may extinguish (on Shabbos) a metal coal in Reshus ha'Rabim, in order that people will not be harmed (normally, this is forbidden mid'Rabanan), but not a coal of wood (for this is forbidden mid'Oraisa);

1.If he held like R. Shimon, he should permit extinguishing even a wooden coal (this is Melachah she'Einah Tzerichah l'Gufah, it is forbidden only mid'Rabanan)!

(c)Answer: He holds like R. Shimon regarding Davar she'Eino Miskaven, but he is Mechayev for Melachah she'Einah Tzerichah l'Gufah like R. Yehudah.

(d)(Ravina): Shmuel teaches that Isurim mid'Rabanan are permitted to prevent damage to the public - therefore, if a thorn is in Reshus ha'Rabim, one may carry it less than four Amos at a time [to the side of Reshus ha'Rabim];

1.If it is in a Karmelis, one may carry it [to where people do not walk] without stopping.

2)MIXING HOT AND COLD WATER

(a)(Mishnah): But one may put...

(b)(Beraisa - Beis Shamai): One may pour hot water into cold water, but not vice-versa;

(c)Beis Hillel permit both of these.

(d)This refers to a cup (a Kli Sheni used for drinking, people do not make it so hot);

1.However, in a bath (Tosfos - a Kli Sheni; alternatively, a Kli Rishon - Tosfos ha'Rosh), one may put only hot into cold [for people make it very hot].

(e)R. Shimon ben Menasiyah forbids.

(f)(Rav Yosef): A bucket (used for bathing) is like a bath.

(g)Rejection (Abaye - R. Chiya's Beraisa): A bucket is not like a bath.

(h)Question: How could Rav Yosef have thought that a bucket is like a bath - Rav Nachman taught that the Halachah follows R. Shimon [ben Menasiyah, who forbids mixing bath water in every case] - it there no way to bathe in hot water on Shabbos?! (Water on the fire is too hot, one must mix it with cold water; it is unfeasible to wait until it cools down.)

(i)Answer: [Tosfos - Rav Yosef thought that] R. Shimon does not discuss the Seifa (a bath), rather, the Reisha - Beis Hillel permit hot into cold and cold into hot;

1.R. Shimon forbids [cold into hot].

(j)Question: Does R. Shimon hold like Beis Shamai?!

(k)Answer: No, he says that there is no argument about this (all forbid cold into hot).

(l)(Rav Huna brei d'Rav Yehoshua): I saw that Rava was not particular about the Kli (Rishon or Sheni) - he relied on R. Chiya's Beraisa:

1.(R. Chiya - Beraisa): One may put a flask of water into a bucket, whether hot into cold or vice-versa.

(m)Question (Rav Huna): Perhaps this is permitted only because a Kli separates the hot and cold water!

(n)Answer (Rav Ashi): The Beraisa permits pouring a flask of water into a bucket, whether hot into cold or vice-versa.

3)WHAT COOKS SALT AND SPICES?

(a)(Mishnah): If one removed a frying pan or pot from the fire, one may not put spices into it [on Shabbos]; (R. Akiva Eiger - Rashi suggests that Bein ha'Shemashos this is permitted - perhaps he holds [like Tosfos] that cooking spices is not forbidden mid'Oraisa, and our Mishnah is like Rebbi, who permits Shevus (Rabbinic decrees of Shabbos) Bein ha'Shemashos.)

(b)One may put spices into a bowl (a Kli Sheni).

(c)R. Yehudah says, one may put [spices] into any [food], except for one with vinegar or brine.

(d)(Gemara) Question: What does R. Yehudah discuss?

1.Perhaps he discusses the Reisha (putting spices in a Kli Rishon off the fire, compared to the first Tana), he is lenient;

2.Perhaps he discusses the Seifa (putting in a Kli Sheni), he is stringent.

(e)Answer (Beraisa - R. Yehudah): One may put [spices] into any simmering pot or pan, except for one with vinegar or brine.

(f)(Rav Yosef): Salt is like spices, [according to the first Tana] it is cooked only in a Kli Rishon, not in a Kli Sheni.

(g)Version #1 - Objection (Abaye - R. Chiya's Beraisa): Salt is unlike spices, it is cooked even in a Kli Sheni.

(h)The Beraisa also argues with Rav Nachman:

1.(Rav Nachman bar Yitzchak): Salt needs much cooking, like ox meat (it gets cooked only in a Kli Rishon).

(i)Version #2 - Objection (Abaye - R. Chiya's Beraisa): Salt is unlike spices, it is not cooked even in a Kli Rishon [off the fire].

(j)This supports Rav Nachman:

1.(Rav Nachman bar Yitzchak): Salt needs much cooking, like ox meat

4)FOR WHAT WE MAY MOVE A KLI

(a)(Mishnah): One may not put a Kli under a Ner to catch dripping oil;

(b)If one put a Kli there from before Shabbos, it is permitted;

1.One may not benefit from the oil, for it is nor prepared [for use on Shabbos, it is Muktzeh].

(c)(Gemara - Rav Chisda): Even though it was taught that one may not put a Kli under a hen to catch an egg it will lay (e.g. it is about to lay an egg on an incline, it is prone to roll and break), one may put a Kli over an egg to prevent it from getting broken.

(d)Question: What is the reason?

(e)Answer #1 (Rabah): He holds that hens often lay eggs in wasteheaps (where they are in danger of getting broken), they rarely lay eggs on inclines;

1.Version #1 (Rashi): Chachamim permitted moving a Kli to save [something Muktzeh, like an egg laid on Shabbos] in a common case, not in an unusual case.

2.Version #2 (Tosfos): Chachamim permitted exertion to prevent a loss in a common case, not in an unusual case.

(f)Question #1 (Abaye - Beraisa): If a barrel [of Tevel - Tosfos deletes this from the text] broke on the roof, one may bring a Kli underneath it [to catch the contents].

(g)Answer: The Beraisa discusses a new barrel [it is prone to break].

(h)Question #2 (Mishnah): One may put a Kli under a Ner to catch sparks.

(i)Answer: A Ner often emits sparks.
