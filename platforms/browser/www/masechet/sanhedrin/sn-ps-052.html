1)R. YISHMAEL'S OPINION (cont.)

(a)Question: How does R. Yishmael expound "Es Aviha Hi Mechaleles"?

(b)Answer: He expounds like R. Meir.

1.(Beraisa - R. Meir): "Es Aviha Hi Mechaleles" teaches that we no longer treat her father honorably (like other Kohanim):

2.If we used to treat him like someone Kodesh, we treat him like Chulin. If we used honor him, we disgrace him;

3.We say 'cursed is the one who fathered such a girl, who raised such a girl.

(c)(Rav Ashi): Even if a Rasha's father is a Tzadik, we call him a Rasha ben Rasha. This is like R. Meir.

(d)(Mishnah): This is the Mitzvah of stoning.

(e)Question: Why does it say 'this is the Mitzvah of stoning?' (The Mishnah listed all the Misos!)

(f)Answer: The previous Perek taught that after the final verdict, he is taken to be stoned. Beis ha'Skilah was twice one's height;

1.Since we will next teach about burning, our Mishnah concluded 'this is the Mitzvah of stoning.'

2)THE MITZVAH OF BURNING

(a)(Mishnah): For the Mitzvah of burning, we insert him in manure up to his knees. We put a hard garment inside a soft garment, and wrap them around his neck. The witnesses pull in both directions, making his mouth open. One lights a Pesilah (this usually means a wick) and throws it into his mouth. This burns his innards.

(b)R. Yehudah says, perhaps they will strangle him, and the Mitzvah will not be fulfilled!

1.Rather, we use tongs to force his mouth open, and throw in the Pesilah as above.

(c)R. Eliezer b'Rebbi Tzadok says, a case occurred in which a Bas Kohen was Mezanah. They surrounded her with bundles of twigs and burned her.

(d)Chachamim: This was incorrect. The Beis Din was not learned.

(e)(Gemara) Question: What is 'Pesilah'?

(f)Answer (Rav Masnah): It is (molten) lead.

(g)Question: What is the source of this?

(h)Answer #1: We learn a Gezerah Shavah "Sereifah-Sereifah" from Korach's congregation. Just like there the Neshamah was burned, but not the body (this will be explained), also (Misas) Sereifah.

(i)Answer #2 (R. Elazar): We learn a Gezerah Shavah "Sereifah-Sereifah" from Nadav and Avihu. Just like there the Neshamah was burned, and not the body (this will be explained), also Sereifah.

(j)Question: How does the first opinion know that in Korach's congregation only the Neshamah was burned, and not the body?

(k)Answer: It says "Es Machtos ha'Chata'im Ha'elu b'Nafshosam" - their Neshamos were burned, and not their bodies.

1.R. Elazar says, a regular fire consumed them (with their bodies);

2."Ha'Chata'im Ha'elu b'Nafshosam" teaches that they were burned due to their Neshamos.

3.(Reish Lakish): "B'Chanfei La'agei Ma'og Charok Alai Shineimo" - due to flattery of food (Korach gave food and drink to people, therefore they supported him), the minister of Gehinom gnashed his teeth at them.

(l)Question: How does R. Elazar know that only the Neshamos of Nadav and Avihu were burned, but not their bodies?

(m)Answer: "Va'Yamusu" - it was like (regular) death (their bodies remained intact).

1.The first opinion holds that a regular fire consumed them. "Va'Yamusu" teaches that the fire started form their insides, like (regular) death (which results from something internal).

2.(Beraisa - Aba Yosi ben Dosta'i): Two strings of fire came from the Kodesh ha'Kodoshim. Each split into two and entered the nostrils of one of them and killed him.

3.Question (against the first opinion): It says "va'Tochal Osam"! (If fire consumed them, what is excluded?)

4.Answer: It consumed them, but not their garments.

(n)Question: Why don't we learn from bulls (whose blood is sprinkled in the Kodesh ha'Kodoshim) that are burned? They are truly burned!

(o)Answer: It is better to learn from people (Korach's congregation or Nadav and Avihu). They resemble Sereifah of Misas Beis Din in these ways:

1.They pertain to people, they pertain to sinners, Neshamos are taken, and Pigul does not apply.

2.Question: Rather, we should learn from bulls. They are burned according to Halachah, and they apply to all generations!

3.Answer: There are more similarities to people.

(p)Question: Why didn't the first opinion learn from Nadav and Avihu?

(q)Answer: He holds that they were truly burned.

(r)Question: Let us learn from them that indeed, we truly burn people!

(s)Answer (Rav Nachman): "V'Ohavta l'Re'acha Kamocha" - choose a nice death for him.

(t)Question: If we learn from there, why do we need the Gezerah Shavah?

(u)Answer: One might have thought that if only the Neshamah is burned but not the body, this is not considered burning. The Gezerah Shavah teaches that it is.

1.One could fulfill "v'Ohavta l'Re'acha Kamocha" by making a great fire that will burn the person very quickly.

3)NADAV AND AVIHU

(a)Moshe and Aharon were walking. Nadav and Avihu were in back of them, and all of Yisrael were in back of them.

1.Nadav (to Avihu): When will these elders (Moshe and Aharon) die? Then, we will lead the generation!

2.Hash-m: We shall see who will die first!

(b)(Rav Papa): This is like people say 'many old camels carry the skins of young camels' (that died because they were not trained to bear a burden).

1.After the Chacham talks with him (about secular matters), the Chacham appears like a silver flask;

2.After the Chacham benefits from him, he appears like a broken earthenware flask, which cannot be fixed.

(d)A certain Bas Kohen was Mezanah. Rav Chama bar Tuvya burned her in a fire of sticks.

(e)Rav Yosef: He made two mistakes. He erred about Rav Masnah's teaching (that the Pesilah for Sereifah is molten lead), and about a Beraisa:

1.(Beraisa): "U'Vosa El ha'Kohanim ha'Leviyim v'El ha'Shofet Asher Yihyeh ba'Yomim ha'Hem" - we judge capital cases only at a time when there are (Shoftim, i.e. the Sanhedrin in Lishkas ha'Gazis, near the) Kohanim.

(f)(Mishnah - R. Eliezer b'Rebbi Tzadok): A case occurred in which a Bas Kohen was Mezanah... (they truly burned her. Chachamim said, the Beis Din was not learned.)

(g)(Rav Yosef): It was a Beis Din of Tzedukim.

(h)Question: Was this really Chachamim's rebuttal of R. Eliezer?!

1.(Beraisa - R. Eliezer b'Rebbi Tzadok): I remember when I was a boy on my father's shoulder, that a Bas Kohen was Mezanah. They surrounded her with bundles of twigs and burned her.

2.Chachamim: We do not bring proof from (something witnessed by) a minor (even after he matures).

(i)Answer: He saw this on two occasions. (Chachamim rejected his two testimonies for different reasons.)

(j)Question: Which did he testify about first?

1.If he first testified about the one he saw in adulthood (and Chachamim said that the Beis Din was not learned), he would not bother to testify later about what he saw as a child!

(k)Answer: He first testified about what he saw when he was a child. Chachamim said that we do not bring proof from a minor;

1.He saw another case when he was an adult, and 3sChachamim said that the Beis Din was not learned.

4)BEHEADING

(a)(Mishnah): Beheading - they behead him (from a standing position) like kingdoms do.

(b)R. Yehudah: That is disgraceful! Rather, we rest his head on a block and cut it with a chopping knife.

(c)Chachamim: That is the most disgraceful death!

(d)(Gemara - Beraisa - R. Yehudah): I agree that this is disgraceful, but we cannot kill like the kingdom - "uv'Chukoseihem Lo Selechu"!

(e)Chachamim: Since the Torah teaches that a sword should be used, we did not learn from the Nochrim.

1.Surely, anything learned from Torah is not forbidden due to "uv'Chukoseihem Lo Selechu";

2.(Beraisa): We burn after a king dies. This is not forbidden due to 'Darchei ha'Emori' (doing like the Kena'anim).

3.Question: This should be forbidden due to "uv'Chukoseihem Lo Selechu"!

4.Answer: Since we learn from Torah, "uv'Misrefos Avosecha...", it is permitted.

(f)(Mishnah): The following are beheaded: a murderer and people of an Ir ha'Nidachas.

1.Regarding Ir ha'Nidachas, it explicitly says "l'Fi Charev";

(g)Question: What is the source that a murderer is beheaded by a sword?

(h)Answer (Beraisa): "Nakom Yinakem" does not specify which Misah;

1.Therefore, it says "Cherev Nokemes Nekam Bris" to teach that it refers to a sword.

(i)Question: Perhaps we stab with a sword!

(j)Answer: It says "l'Fi Charev" (the edge of a sword, and not the point).

(k)Question: Perhaps we cut the person lengthwise!

(l)Answer (Rav Nachman): "V'Ohavta l'Re'acha Kamocha" - choose a nice death for him.

(m)Question: "Nakom Yinakem" refers to one who killed a slave. What is the source that one who killed a Yisrael is beheaded? (There, it says "Yumas", which refers to choking.)

(n)Answer #1: We learn from a Kal va'Chomer. One who killed a slave is beheaded, and all the more so one who killed a Yisrael!

(o)Question: This is like the opinion that beheading is more severe than choking. like the opinion that choking is more severe, how can we answer?

(p)Answer #2 (Beraisa): "V'Atah Teva'er ha'Dam ha'Naki mi'Kirbecha" - murderers (about whom it similarly says "u'Bi'arta ha'Ra mi'Kirbecha") are equated to Eglah Arufah, which is killed by a knife through the neck.

(q)Question: If we learn from Eglah Arufah, we should use a chopping knife and cut from the back of the neck!

(r)Answer (Rav Nachman): "V'Ohavta l'Re'acha Kamocha" - choose a nice death for him.

5)CHOKING

(a)(Mishnah): The Mitzvah of choking: we insert him in manure up to his knees, we put a hard garment inside a soft garment, and wrap them around his neck. The witnesses pull in both directions, until he dies.

(b)(Gemara - Beraisa): "Ish (a man who transgresses adultery)" excludes a minor;

1."Asher Yin'af Es Eshes Ish" excludes a minor's wife (i.e. a minor cannot Mekadesh);

2."Eshes Re'ehu" excludes a Nochri's wife (a Nochri cannot be Mekadesh).

3."Mos Yumas" - he is choked.

4.Question: Perhaps this refers to a different Misah!

5.Answer #1 (R. Yoshiyah): Whenever the Torah does not specify which Misah, we give the lightest (choking).

6.Answer #2 (R. Yonason): Unspecified Misah refers to choking, even though it is not the lightest (this will be explained).

7.Rebbi: He learns from Misah bi'Ydei Shamayim (which is also unspecified);

i.Just like Misah bi'Ydei Shamayim (Aruch - natural death; Rashi - Onan's death) is not noticeable on the body, also Misas Beis Din.

8.Question: Perhaps it refers to burning, which does not mar the body!

9.Answer: Since the Torah says that a Bas Kohen is burned for Zenus, this implies that a Bas Yisrael (about whom it says "Mos Yumas") is not burned.
