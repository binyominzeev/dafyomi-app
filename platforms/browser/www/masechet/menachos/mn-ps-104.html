BLOOD OF A NEVEILAH

(a)

Question: What was the conclusion?

(b)

Answer (Rav Yosef): Rebbi's house relied on R. Yehudah for rulings. He taught as follows:

1.

(Beraisa - R. Yehudah): The following six laws are leniencies of Beis Shamai and stringencies of Beis Hillel. (Our Gemara brings only the one relevant to our discussion.)

i.

Beis Shamai are Metaher Dam Neveilah. Beis Hillel are Metamei.

2)

A NEDAVAH OF WINE

(a)

(Mishnah): We do not bring a Nedavah of one, two or five Lugim of wine (for this amount is never brought for (any combination of) Nesachim;

(b)

We may bring three Lugim (the size of Niskei Keves), four (like Niskei Ayil), six or any greater number of Lugim. (Any multiple of three (no less than six) corresponds to a whole number of Nesachim for lambs. If a number is one (or two) more than a multiple of three, we substitute one (or two) ram(s) for one (or two) lamb(s).)

(c)

(Gemara) Question: Are Nesachim fixed? (Rashi - if one vowed to bring a certain amount, must he bring it all at once? Tosfos - if he vowed, he must immediately bring an amount that can be brought, no less than his vow. We ask about one who said 'this wine is a Nedavah.')

1.

Question: What is the case?

2.

Answer: If Reuven brought five Lugim:

i.

If we say that Nesachim are not fixed, he can offer four. The extra Log will be Nedavah (it itself, or wine bought with its redemption money will be combined with other wine for Niskei Tzibur);

ii.

If Nesachim are fixed, it cannot be offered until a sixth Log is added.

(d)

Answer #1 (Abaye - Mishnah) There were six boxes for Nedavah (money to buy Nidvos Tzibur.)

1.

Question: Why were there six?

2.

Answer: They correspond to six Mosaros, of Chatas, Asham, Asham Nazir, Asham Metzora, Kinim (Chatas ha'Of and Olas ha'Of) and Minchas Chotei.

3.

If Nesachim are not fixed, there should have been a seventh box for Nedavah, i.e. Mosar Nesachim!

(e)

Rejection: No, there are boxes only for money of Mosaros that must be saved for Nedavah;

1.

Mosar Nesachim is very common. Usually it can be joined to other people's Mosar Nesachim and (it itself is) offered the same day. We need not (redeem it and) store the money.

(f)

Answer #2 (Rava - Beraisa): "Ezrach" teaches that one may bring Nesachim for Nedavah;

1.

Question: How much does one bring?

2.

Answer: He brings three Lugim (equal to the smallest Nesech of any animal).

3.

Question: What is the source to permit bringing more?

4.

Answer: It says "Yihyeh".

5.

Suggestion: Perhaps he may bring less?

6.

Rejection It says "Kachah".

7.

Question: The Beraisa expounded that one may 'bring more.' What does this mean?

i.

It cannot mean bringing four or six. No verse is needed for them, for they correspond to Niskei Eilim and Parim!

8.

Answer: Rather, it means that he may bring five, i.e. Nesachim are not fixed.

(g)

Question (Rav Ashi - Mishnah): We do not bring a Nedavah of one, two or five Lugim of wine;

1.

Presumably, the law of five is like that of (one or) two, i.e. one cannot bring even part of it!

(h)

Answer: We need not say so. Their laws can be different! (One who vowed five brings four now...)

(i)

(Abaye): If Nesachim are not fixed, the law is clear. (Whatever may be offered, we offer. The rest is Nedavah);

(j)

Question: If Nesachim are fixed, surely any amount from six until 10 is offered;

1.

However, if one brings 11, what is the law?

104b----------------------------------------104b

i.

Do we say that he intends to bring Nesachim for two bulls (12), therefore he must bring 12?

ii.

Or, perhaps he intends to bring Nesachim for two rams and a sheep?

iii.

Is it normal to bring Nesachim corresponding to different animals (more than just one of each), or not? (If it is normal, surely this is his intent. We would not suspect that he brought an incomplete Nesech.)

(k)

This question is not resolved.

3)

A NEDAVAH OF OIL

(a)

(Mishnah - R. Akiva): One may bring a Nedavah of wine, but not of oil;

(b)

R. Tarfon says, one may bring a Nedavah (even) of oil.

(c)

R. Tarfon: We find that wine is brought Chovah (i.e. with a Korban) and Nedavah. Likewise oil, which is brought Chovah, may be brought Nedavah!

(d)

R. Akiva: You cannot learn from wine. When it is brought Chovah (i.e. with a Korban), it is offered by itself. When oil is brought Chovah, it is mixed (with the Minchah)!

(e)

Two partners may not bring (a Minchah of) one Isaron, but they may bring a (Behemah for an) Olah, Shelamim or even one bird.

(f)

(Gemara - Rava): We learn from R. Akiva and R. Tarfon that one may offer a Nedavah of Minchas Nesachim every day.

(g)

Objection: This is obvious!

(h)

Answer: One might have thought that the Torah teaches about five kinds of Minchas Nedavah (brought by themselves), and there are no others. Rava teaches that this is when one (vowed to bring a Minchah and) did not specify, but if he specifies to bring Minchas Nesachim, he may do so.

(i)

(Mishnah): Two partners may not bring (one Isaron, but they may bring an Olah, Shelamim or even one bird).

(j)

Question: What is the reason?

(k)

Answer #1: Regarding Minchah it says "Takriv" (singular).

1.

Question: Similarly, regarding Olah it says "Yakriv"!

2.

Answer: Regarding Olah it says "l'Oloseichem" (to permit partnership).

3.

Objection: Similarly, regarding Minchah it says "l'Minchoseichem"!

(l)

Answer #2: Regarding Minchah it says "Nefesh".

(m)

Support (Beraisa - Rebbi): "Asher Yakriv Korbano l'Chol Nidreihem ul'Chol Nidvosam Asher Yakrivu la'Shem" teaches that everything may be brought in partnership. The Torah excludes only Minchah, there it says "Nefesh".

(n)

Question (R. Yitzchak): Why does it say "Nefesh" only regarding Minchah?

(o)

Answer: Usually a poor person brings a Minchah. Hash-m considers it as if he offered his soul!

(p)

Question (R. Yitzchak): Why are there five kinds of Minchas Nedavah with oil?

(q)

Answer: A parable explains this. The king's friend prepared a meal for the king. The king knew that his friend was poor, so he requested that he make five kinds of fried foods, in order that the meal will be pleasing.

PEREK HAREI ALAI ISARON

4)

VAGUE VOWS

(a)

(Mishnah): If one said 'Alai (it is incumbent on me to bring) an Isaron', he must bring (a Minchah of) one Isaron;

(b)

If he said 'Alai Esronim', he must bring two Esronim;

(c)

If he (knows that he) specified how many Esronim he will bring, but does not remember the amount, he must bring 60 Esronim (the largest possible Minchah. If he vowed less, the excess is Nedavah (Rashash. Sefas Emes - he volunteers to increase his vow to 60).)

(d)

If he said 'Alai Minchah', he may bring any (of the five types of) Minchas Nedavah that he wants;

(e)

R. Yehudah says, he must bring Minchas Soles, for this is the choice Minchah.

(f)

If he said '(Alai) Minchah', or 'a type of Minchah', he must bring one Minchah;

(g)

If he said 'Menachos', or 'a type of Menachos', he must bring two Menachos;

(h)

If he specified which type he will bring, but does not remember which, he must bring all five kinds.

(i)

If he vowed 'A Minchah of (a certain number of) Esronim', but does not remember the amount, he must bring 60 Esronim. (We are not concerned lest he vowed more than this, for this is not normal);

(j)

Rebbi says, he must bring Menachos of every size, from one Isaron until 60 (60 Menachos in all).

(k)

(Gemara) Objection: The first law ('Alai Isaron') is obvious!

(l)

Answer: It is taught due to the next law, 'Alai Esronim'.

(m)

Objection: Also this is obvious! 'Esronim' is at least two!

(n)

Answer: It is taught due to the next law, when he specified but does not remember the amount.

(o)

(Mishnah): If he specified but does not remember the amount, he must bring 60 Esronim.

(p)

Question: Who is the Tana of the Mishnah?

(q)

Answer #1 (Chizkiyah): It is not Rebbi, for he says (in the Seifa) that he must bring Menachos of every size, from one until 60.

(r)

Objection (and Answer #2 - R. Yochanan): It is even like Rebbi. The case is, he specified how much he will bring, but he did not vow to bring them in one Kli;

1.

He brings 60 Esronim, each in its own Kli (no matter what he vowed, he is Yotzei. In the Seifa, he specified to bring them in one Kli, therefore he must bring Menachos of every size.)

(s)

(Mishnah): If he said 'Alai Minchah', he may bring any Minchas Nedavah... (R. Yehudah obligates bringing Soles, the choice Minchah.)

(t)

(Beraisa): This (R. Yehudah's opinion) is because the Torah taught about Minchas Soles first.
