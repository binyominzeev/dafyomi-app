1)

KISUY HA'DAM OF KODSHIM (cont.)

(a)

Answer #1: Indeed, we cover blood of Korbanos ha'Of in this way;

1.

The Mishnah teaches that Kisuy ha'Dam does not apply to Kodshim of Bedek ha'Bayis (for they may not be eaten).

2.

Question: Since we can redeem them (and eat them), we should do Kisuy!

3.

Answer: Redemption of animals requires Ha'amadah and Ha'arachah (standing it up before a Kohen for appraisal). This cannot be done after Shechitah.

4.

Question: Like whom is the Mishnah?

i.

Suggestion: It is like R. Meir, who holds that even Kodshim of Bedek ha'Bayis require Ha'amadah and Ha'arachah.

ii.

Rejection: He holds that even if Shechitah does not permit the meat to be eaten, it is considered Shechitah (and we must cover the blood)!

iii.

Suggestion: The Mishnah is like R. Shimon, who says that Shechitah that does not permit is not considered Shechitah (so Kisuy is not needed).

iv.

Rejection: He holds that Kodshei Bedek ha'Bayis do not require Ha'amadah and Ha'arachah!

5.

Answer #1 (Rav Yosef): The Mishnah is like Rebbi;

i.

Rebbi holds like R. Shimon regarding Shechitah that does not permit (it is not considered Shechitah), and like R. Meir regarding redemption of Kodshei Bedek ha'Bayis.

6.

Answer #2: The Mishnah is like R. Shimon. A verse teaches that the blood must be fit to be covered immediately;

i.

"He will spill (the blood) and cover" - the blood must be fit to be covered immediately after Shechitah;

ii.

This excludes Kodshei Bedek ha'Bayis, which need not be covered until after redemption.

iii.

This suggests another answer to Question 4:b (83b).

(b)

Answer #2: The Mishnah exempts also Korbanos from Kisuy, since Kisuy cannot be done until the blood is scraped off the Mizbe'ach. ("He will spill and cover" excludes this.)

(c)

Answer #3 (Mar bar Rav Ashi): The Mishnah exempts also Korbanos from Kisuy. "Chayah Oh Ohf" teaches that Kisuy applies to birds that are like Chayos, i.e. they are not Korbanos.

1.

Suggestion: We should say that the verse discusses species of birds that are like Chayos, i.e. they are invalid for Kodshim;

i.

This would exclude Torim and doves.

2.

Rejection: The verse equates Chayos and birds. Just like Kisuy applies to all (Kosher) species of Chayos, also regarding birds.

2)

TO WHICH ANIMALS THE MITZVAH APPLIES

(a)

Question (Yakov Mina'ah): The Torah gives Simanim of Kosher Behemos. They apply also to Chayos;

1.

We should say that the Torah commands to cover the blood of Chayos, and this applies also to Behemos!

(b)

Answer (Rava): "On the ground you will spill it (Dam of blemished Korbanos, which are Behemos) like water" - just like water need not be covered, also Dam Behemah.

(c)

Question: If blood of blemished Korbanos is equated to water, it should be valid for a Mikveh!

(d)

Answer: "Ach Mayan u'Vor Mikveh Mayim Yihyeh Tahor" - only these (springs and pits of water) are valid Mikva'os, but nothing else.

(e)

Question: The verse should exclude other liquids, which are not called water, but not Dam!

(f)

Answer: There are two exclusions in the verse. It is as if it says 'Mayan Mayim u'Vor Mayim'.

(g)

Question: We should exclude two cases of other liquids, flowing or collected, but not blood, which is called water!

(h)

Answer: There are three exclusions in the verse. It is as if it says 'Mayan Mayim u'Vor Mayim Mikveh Mayim.'

(i)

(Beraisa) Suggestion: Perhaps "Asher Yatzud" teaches that if it was already trapped (e.g. domestic geese and chickens), the Mitzvah does not apply!

(j)

Rejection: "Tzeid" includes all cases.

(k)

Question: If so, why did the Torah say "Asher Yatzud"?

(l)

Answer: This teaches proper conduct, that one should not eat meat regularly, only on occasion (like when one happens to trap an animal).

3)

BEING CAREFUL WITH YOUR MONEY

(a)

(Beraisa): "When Hash-m will expand your borders (... and you will lust to eat meat)" teaches proper behavior, that one should eat meat only when he has a craving for it.

(b)

Suggestion: Perhaps he may buy meat to eat!

(c)

Rejection: "You will slaughter from your cattle and flock".

(d)

Suggestion: Perhaps he may slaughter all his animals!

(e)

Rejection: It says "from your cattle and from your flock", but not all of them.

1.

(R. Eliezer ben Azaryah): If one has one Maneh (100 Zuz, which is less than an average annual income. One who has less than two Manos is considered poor), he should buy one Litra of vegetables. If he has 10 Manos, he should buy fish. If he has 50 Manos, he should buy meat. If he has 100 Manos, he should have meat every day.

2.

Question: How often should people with 10 (and 50) Manos eat fish (and meat)?

3.

Answer: They should eat them only on Shabbos.

4.

(Rav): We should follow R. Eliezer ben Azaryah's teaching.

5.

(R. Yochanan): Rav was healthy. He could conduct like R. Eliezer ben Azaryah. We are not healthy. One who has money should spend it on food.

6.

(Rav Nachman): We are so weak that we should borrow money to buy food.

(f)

"Sheep for your clothing" teaches that one should clothe himself from their wool;

(g)

"The price of a field goats" teaches that one should sell his field, and use the money to buy goats;

1.

they give milk and clothing.)"It is enough (for your sustenance), the milk of goats" - you should not slaughter them.

(h)

"For your bread, for the bread of your household" teaches that your bread comes before that of your household;

(i)

(Mar Zutra brei d'Rav Nachman): "And life for your youths" - do not accustom your children to meat and wine (rather, to simple foods).

(j)

(R. Yochanan): To get rich, one should raise small animals.

(k)

(R. Yochanan): Lukewarm water is more hazardous than water on which witchcraft has been used.

1.

This is only if the warm water was in a metal Kli (not earthenware), without spices, and was never boiled;

1.

4)

KISUY HA'DAM ON SHABBOS OR YOM TOV

(a)

(Rav Eina): If one slaughters (a Chayah or bird) for a Choleh (dangerously sick person) on Shabbos, he must cover the blood.

(b)

Question (Rabah - Beraisa - R. Yosi): We do not slaughter a Koy on Yom Tov. If it was slaughtered, a Kal va'Chomer teaches that we do not do Kisuy;

1.

Bris Milah overrides Shabbos if it is surely the eighth day. If it is a Safek, it does not override even Yom Tov;

2.

Kisuy does not override Shabbos, even for a definite Chayah or bird. All the more so, it does not override Yom Tov for a Safek Chayah!

3.

Objection #1 (Chachamim): Teki'as (sounding the) Shofar (on Rosh Hashanah) in the Gevulim (outside the Mikdash, or Yerushalayim, or the city where the Sanhedrin resides) disproves this. We do not blow on Shabbos even for a definite Mitzvah, but we blow on Yom Tov for a Safek (this will be explained)!

4.

Objection #2 (R. Elazar Hakapar Beribi): Kisuy applies even at times when Milah does not, e.g. the night of Yom Tov, so one cannot learn from Milah that Kisuy does not override Yom Tov for a Safek!

i.

R. Aba: R. Chiya said that he (i.e. anyone) cannot challenge R. Yosi's Kal va'Chomer. R. Elazar Hakapar challenged it! (R. Chiya holds that it is not a proper challenge.)

5.

(Summation of question (b)): It says that Kisuy does not override Shabbos, even for a definite Chayah or bird.

6.

Suggestion: The case is, the Shechitah was for a Choleh.

(c)

Answer: Perhaps the Shechitah was for a healthy person. Since it was forbidden to slaughter, Kisuy is not done.

(d)

Rejection: No, the Shechitah is like Milah (from which R. Yosi learns), which was permitted. (Rav Eina is refuted.)
