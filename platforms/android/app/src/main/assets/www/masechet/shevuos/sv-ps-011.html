1)THE KEDUSHAH OF KETORES

(a)Question (Rabah): If Ketores has only monetary Kedushah, a Tevul Yom (one who immersed from Tum'ah today; he will be fully Tahor at nightfall) should not disqualify it!

1.(Beraisa): If Ketores was put in the grinder, if a Tevul Yom touches it, he disqualifies it.

(b)Suggestion: Perhaps a Tevul Yom disqualifies things with monetary Kedushah!

(c)Rejection (Mishnah): From the time one is Makdish a Minchah (flour-offering), Me'ilah applies to it. When it is put in a Keli Shares (service vessel), it gets more Kedushah. It becomes disqualified if a Tevul Yom or Mechushar Kipurim (one who was Tamei, and his Taharah will be complete when he brings a Korban) touches it, or if left overnight;

1.The latter two laws apply only after it was put in a Keli Shares, but not before!

2.Counter-question (Rav Chisda): If Ketores has Kedushas ha'Guf (it is considered destined to be used for the Avodah), it should be disqualified if left overnight!

i.(Mishnah): Me'ilah applies to the following from the time of Hekdesh: the Kometz (the handful taken from a Minchah and burned on the Altar), frankincense, Ketores, the Minchah of a Kohen, the daily Minchah of an anointed Kohen Gadol, and the Minchah that accompanies an animal Korban;

ii.After they are put in a Keli Shares, they are disqualified if touched by a Tevul Yom or Mechushar Kipurim, or if left overnight.

iii.Inference: Before they are put in a Keli Shares, they are not disqualified if left overnight!

3.Answer (Rabah): Ketores is different. The Torah instructs to make it once for the entire year, so mid'Oraisa it is not disqualified if left overnight (Tosfos - until put in the pan in which it is burned).

2)HEKDESH BUYS ON CONDITION

(a)Question: We still have not answered the question (4:b Daf 10b. Why can Tam Korbanos (or Ketores) be redeemed? Did the Kedushah vanish?!)

(b)Answer (Rabah): Beis Din (Hekdesh) purchases things needed for the Avodah on condition that anything that is not needed does not get Kedushas ha'Guf.

(c)Question (Abaye): You yourself taught that if one is Makdish a male animal to (sell it and use the money to) buy an Olah, the animal gets Kedushas ha'Guf!

(d)Answer (Rabah): That is because it was Hukdash for something for which it is Ra'uy (appropriate);

1.However, if it was Hukdash to (sell it and use the money to) buy Nesachim (the accompanying Minchah), for which it is not Ra'uy, it does not get Kedushas ha'Guf.

(e)Question (Abaye - Mishnah - R. Yehudah): If the (inner) bull or goat of Yom Kipur was lost, and another was taken in its place and the lost animal was found, we leave it to die;

11b----------------------------------------11b

1.The same apples to goats that atone for (a mistaken ruling of the Sanhedrin that caused most of Yisrael to transgress) idolatry.

(f)R. Elazar and R. Shimon say that it is left to graze until it gets a Mum (blemish). It is redeemed, and the money goes to Nedavah (to buy Olos for the Tzibur to bring when the Altar is idle);

1.(If an individual's Chatas cannot be offered, it must die, but) the Tzibur's Chatas is never left to die.

2.Summation of question: If Hekdesh purchases things for the Avodah on condition, we should redeem it Tam!

(g)Answer (Rabah): These are uncommon Korbanos (and it is rare that one would be lost). Hekdesh does not stipulate about them.

(h)Question (Abaye): It is uncommon that we will redeem a red heifer because a nicer one was found, yet Hekdesh stipulates about this!

1.(Beraisa): A red heifer is redeemed if it is disqualified in any way, if it died, if it was slaughtered (in the wrong place), or if a nicer red heifer was found;

2.If it was slaughtered in the proper place (facing the entrance to the Heichal), it may never be redeemed.

(i)Answer #1 (Rabah): The red heifer is different, since it is Hekdesh Bedek ha'Bayis (it is not offered on the Altar), so even if Hekdesh did not stipulate, it can be redeemed Tam.

1.Question: Why can it be redeemed after it died or was slaughtered? It must be stood up and evaluated!

2.Answer: The Beraisa is R. Shimon, who says that only Kedushas ha'Guf must be stood up and evaluated (before redemption), and not Bedek ha'Bayis.

3.Question (end of the Beraisa): If it was slaughtered in the proper place, it may never be redeemed.

i.(Beraisa - R. Shimon): The red heifer receives Tum'ah because there was a time when it was Ra'uy (to be eaten).

ii.(Reish Lakish): He holds that it was Ra'uy after slaughter, for if a nicer one was found, we would have redeemed the first one. (This contradicts the Beraisa!)

(j)Answer #2 (Rabah): Rather, even though it is uncommon to find a nicer red heifer, Hekdesh stipulates about this because red heifers are very expensive.

(k)(Beraisa): If a red heifer died (without slaughter), it is redeemed.

(l)Question: We do not redeem Kodshim to feed them to dogs!

(m)Answer (Rav Mesharshiya): It is redeemed for its skin.

(n)Question: Does Hekdesh make the Kedushah conditional just so if it dies, we can redeem it for the skin?!

(o)Answer (Rav Kahana): Even the ear of an (expensive animal such as a) camel is worthwhile.

(p)Question (Mishnah - Rabanan): If a goat designated for one of these three atonements was lost, and it was found after a substitute was offered, may the found goat be used for another of the three atonements?

1.R. Shimon: Yes.

2.Rabanan: If it was originally designated for one atonement, how can it atone for something else?!

3.R. Shimon: They all atone for Tum'ah in the Mikdash or with Kodshim. This is essentially what it was designated for!

4.Summation of question: Why didn't R. Shimon answer that Hekdesh buys things for the Avodah on condition?

(q)Answer: R. Shimon holds that Hekdesh does not buy things on condition.

1.(Rav Idi bar Avin): R. Shimon forbids redeeming Tam lambs for the Tamid that were not used by Nisan. Chachamim permit this.

(r)Question: Who are the Chachamim who argue with R. Shimon (and say that Hekdesh purchases on condition)?
