1)NEZIRUS IMPOSED ON A SON

(a)Answer: Rather, Chanina said 'I am a Nazir due to my father, or due to myself' (and stipulated based on whether or not he was an adult).

1.If he brought two hairs from the beginning (before his father made him a Nazir), he is a Nazir due to his own acceptance;

2.If he brought two hairs at the end (after finishing Nezirus), he was a Nazir because of his father.

(b)Question: We should be concerned lest he brought two hairs in the middle (of Nezirus. How can he bring Korbanos?)

1.Such a question is not difficult according to R. Yosi b'Rebbi Yehudah. He holds that the Halachah applies until Onas ha'Nedarim. If the father's Nezirus began, it continues after the son reaches Onas ha'Nedarim.

2.But Rebbi holds that the Halachah applies until he brings two hairs. At this time, the father's Nezirus ceases!

(c)Answer: Indeed, Rebbi holds that he cannot bring Korbanos until he observes another 30 days for his own (Safek) Nezirus (due to the concern that his father's Nezirus did not finish).

2)GILU'ACH WITH THE FATHER'S MONEY

(a)(Mishnah - R. Yosi): A son can be Megale'ach with his father's money (that was designated for the Korbanos he brings when he shaves), but a daughter cannot';

(b)Question: What is the case?

1.If Yakov was a Nazir and designated Stam money for his Korbanos, and died, and his son Levi said 'I accept Nezirus on condition to be Megale'ach with my father's money, he cannot! Rather, the money goes to Nedavah (for Olos Tzibur).

(c)Answer: Rather, he may be Megale'ach with Yakov's money when they were both Nezirim, and Yakov designated money and died. (Many texts are reversed, and permit Gilu'ach with Yakov's money only when Levi vowed after Yakov died. The Gemara below (j) supports them. Rashi switches the text below to conform to our text here.)

(d)(Gemara): What is the source that only a son may shave on his father's money?

(e)Answer (R. Yochanan): It is a tradition from Sinai.

(f)Objection: Why is a tradition needed? This is obvious! The son inherits his father, not the daughter!

(g)Answer: The tradition is needed for a girl with no brothers;

1.One might have thought that the Halachah applies to any heir. The tradition teaches that it does not.

2.Or, do we learn from inheritance, and each may be Megale'ach with half?
